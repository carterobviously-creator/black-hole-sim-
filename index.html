About Black Holes

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing—no particles or even electromagnetic radiation such as light—can escape from it.

Spaghettification is the vertical stretching and horizontal compression of objects into long, thin shapes due to extreme tidal forces.

This simulation shows how different objects would be affected as they approach a black hole's event horizon.
